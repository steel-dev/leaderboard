# Humanity's Last Exam

Humanity's Last Exam (HLE) is a multi-modal benchmark introduced in January 2025, designed to be the final closed-ended academic benchmark with broad subject coverage at the frontier of human knowledge. Developed globally by subject-matter experts, HLE consists of 2,500 questions spanning dozens of subjects including mathematics, humanities, and the natural sciences. Questions are formatted as multiple-choice and short-answer suitable for automated grading, each with a known, unambiguous, and easily verifiable solution that cannot be quickly answered via internet retrieval.

Evaluation uses exact-match automated grading against verified ground-truth answers. State-of-the-art LLMs demonstrate low accuracy and poor calibration on HLE, highlighting a significant gap between current model capabilities and the expert human frontier. At release, frontier models scored well below expert human performance, in stark contrast to benchmarks like MMLU where LLMs now exceed 90% accuracy. The benchmark is specifically designed so that questions resist retrieval-based shortcuts.

HLE matters because it addresses benchmark saturation, the central problem facing LLM evaluation today. By sourcing questions from domain experts at the boundary of human knowledge, it provides meaningful headroom for measuring future model progress. The dataset is publicly available to inform both research and policymaking.
